Why Believe

Six Reasons to Trust in God

These are not blind leaps of faith — they are invitations grounded in reason, history, and lived experience.

✦

Creation Points to a Creator

The universe operates on precise mathematical laws, fine-tuned constants, and breathtaking order. The probability of this arising by chance alone points powerfully to intentional design.

⟡

A Moral Compass Within You

Every civilization across history has held a deep sense of right and wrong. This universal moral law, written in every human heart, reflects a Lawgiver greater than ourselves.

◈

History Changed by One Life

No figure in history has reshaped civilization more profoundly than Jesus Christ — his teachings, resurrection accounts, and the transformed lives of his followers remain compelling evidence.

✧

Scripture Stands the Test of Time

The Bible's historical accuracy, fulfilled prophecies, and archaeological corroboration are unmatched by any ancient document. It speaks to every culture, every era, and every human heart.

❋

Millions of Transformed Lives

Across centuries and cultures, people who encounter God report profound inner change — freedom from addiction, healed relationships, restored purpose, and an unexplainable peace.

How to Follow

Beginning Your Walk with God

You do not need to be perfect, polished, or prepared. You only need to be willing.

1

Acknowledge and Seek

Begin with an honest prayer. Tell God exactly where you are — your doubts, your brokenness, your questions. He already knows, and He welcomes the conversation.

2

Read the Word Daily

Start with the Gospel of John. The Bible is not a rulebook — it is a love letter. Read a few verses each morning and let them shape your day.

3

Speak to God in Prayer

Prayer is simply honest conversation. There is no formula required. Speak as you would to the most understanding, loving person you can imagine — because that is exactly who you are speaking to.

4

Find a Community of Faith

The journey of faith is not meant to be walked alone. A good church provides belonging, accountability, and wisdom. Seek one that teaches love, truth, and grace in equal measure.

5

Trust the Process

Faith grows slowly and unevenly. Doubt is not the opposite of faith — it is part of it. Keep showing up. Keep asking. God is patient, and He does not give up on you.
